Course Objectives

  1. Develope awareness and familiarity with the fundamental conservation laws that govern continuum mechanics.
  2. Develope a understanding of fundamentals of elasticity, plasticity, and viscoplasticity for developing or using advanced constitutive models.
  3. Develope an understanding of fundamentals of the finite element method to solve quasistatic and dynamic problems.
  4. Develope an understanding of fundamentals of time integration schemes and their limitations in convergence and stability of dynamic solutions.
  5. Develope a practical understanding of the verification, calibration, and validation processes of continuum numerical models

Learning outcomes

  1. Describe and explain the fundamental conservation laws that govern continuum mechanics.
  2. Describe and explain the fundamentals of elasticity, plasticity, and viscoplasticity or successfully calibrate constitutive models for engineering applications.
  3. Develope or make adequate use of finite element codes to solve the governing equations of continuum mechanics
  4. Describe and select appropriate time integration schemes for solving dynamic problems accounting for solution stability
  5. Verify, calibrate, and validate continuum numerical models.

Why modelling continuum mechanics?

The laws of continuum bodies are written in terms of partial differential equations. These equations are difficult to solve analytically. Thus, we need to solve them numerically provided some boundary conditions. We call this problems Boundary value problems

Numerical modelling

Numerical modelling is a multidisplinary field that involves mathematics, physics, computer science, and engineering.

Modelling involves simplifying a phenomena making appropriate assumptions.
